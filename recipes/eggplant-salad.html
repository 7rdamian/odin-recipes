Eggplant Salad

Description

A flavorful and aromatic salad made with roasted or grilled eggplants, finely chopped onions, garlic, and fresh herbs like parsley or dill. The eggplants are mashed to a creamy texture and mixed with olive oil and lemon juice or vinegar, creating a rich, smoky taste with a tangy kick. Served chilled or at room temperature, this eggplant salad is a perfect appetizer or side dish, offering a delightful blend of earthiness and zest.

Ingredients

  • 2 large eggplants
  • 1 small red or white onion, finely chopped
  • 1–2 cloves garlic, minced (optional, to taste)
  • 1–2 tablespoons lemon juice or vinegar (to taste)
  • 3 fresh parsley or dill, finely chopped (optional, for garnish)
  • salt, pepper etc

Preparation

  1. Begin by roasting two large eggplants until their skins are blackened and the insides are soft. This can be done over an open flame, on a grill, or in a hot oven. Once roasted, allow the eggplants to cool slightly, then peel off the charred skin and let the flesh drain for 10–15 minutes to remove excess liquid.

  2. Finely chop or mash the drained eggplant until smooth. In a bowl, combine it with one finely chopped onion, a minced garlic clove (optional), and gradually mix in olive oil. Add lemon juice or vinegar for a tangy flavor, and season with salt and pepper to taste.

  3. Refrigerate the salad for at least 30 minutes to let the flavors blend. Before serving, garnish with chopped fresh parsley or dill. Serve it chilled or at room temperature as a dip, spread, or side dish.
